FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my organisation is ready for machine learning? ▾

Your organisation is ready when you have clean, accessible historical data and a clear business question that would benefit from prediction or pattern recognition. We conduct a rapid ML readiness assessment in week one — evaluating data quality, volume, infrastructure gaps, and team capability — so you know exactly where you stand before committing resources.

How much data do I need for a successful ML project? ▾

It depends on the use case, but most enterprise ML projects need at least 12-24 months of historical data with consistent quality. Some techniques like transfer learning can work with less. We don't believe in feeding you a generic answer — our feasibility assessment will tell you exactly what data you have, what's missing, and whether it's sufficient for your target use case.

How do you maintain and update ML models after deployment? ▾

We set up MLOps pipelines with automated retraining triggers, model versioning, performance monitoring, and drift detection. Models degrade over time as data patterns shift — our monitoring dashboards alert you before accuracy drops below acceptable thresholds, and automated retraining pipelines ensure models stay current without manual intervention.

What are the risks of generative AI in enterprise settings? ▾

The main risks include hallucination (confident incorrect outputs), data leakage (sending sensitive data to third-party LLMs), and lack of explainability. We mitigate these through retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) architectures, strict guardrails, human-in-the-loop approval workflows, and on-premise model deployment options that keep your data secure and compliant.

Should we build a custom model or use an off-the-shelf solution? ▾

Off-the-shelf solutions (pre-trained APIs, SaaS tools) are great for common use cases like document classification, sentiment analysis, or generic chatbots. Custom models make sense when you need proprietary predictions unique to your business — like property valuations, rent forecasting, or portfolio risk scoring. We'll recommend the most cost-effective approach based on your specific requirements.
