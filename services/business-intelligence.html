FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between BI and data analytics? ▾

BI focuses on what happened — reporting, dashboards, and KPIs that track performance against targets. Data analytics explores why it happened and what might happen next through statistical analysis and modelling. A mature organisation needs both: BI for operational visibility and analytics for strategic insight. Our BI programmes establish this foundation.

How do I enable self-service analytics without losing control? ▾

Self-service analytics succeeds when you separate the data layer from the presentation layer. We build governed semantic models with certified data definitions and row-level security, then let business users explore within those guardrails. This gives them freedom to create their own reports while ensuring everyone works from the same trusted data foundation.

What does good data governance look like in practice? ▾

Good governance is invisible to end users but rigorous behind the scenes. It includes clear data ownership, automated quality checks, business glossary definitions, data lineage tracking, and stewardship processes. The key is making governance enable rather than restrict — when done well, your teams move faster because they trust the data they're working with.

How do I choose the right BI tool for my organisation? ▾

We evaluate tools against your specific needs — user base size, technical capability, data volume, integration requirements, and budget. Power BI excels for Microsoft-centric organisations, Tableau for visual analytics, and Looker for cloud-native environments. We're vendor-agnostic and run structured proof-of-concepts to validate each tool against your real use cases before recommending.

How do you drive BI adoption and measure ROI? ▾

Adoption doesn't happen by accident. We design change management programmes including executive sponsorship, power user networks, training curricula, and communications campaigns. We track adoption metrics (active users, report views, data literacy scores) alongside business outcomes (faster decisions, reduced reporting effort, improved data accuracy) to demonstrate measurable ROI.
